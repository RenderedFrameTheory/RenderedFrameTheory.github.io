Rendered Frame Theory (RFT)
Mathematical Foundations & Formulas

This page documents the mathematical framework of Rendered Frame Theory (RFT), including prediction methods, symbolic definitions, and render-phase calculations used to forecast magnetic, solar, seismic, and atmospheric anomalies.

Example Core Formula: Observer Render Phase

Ω_obs(t) = ∇φ · (τ_eff / Δt_r)

Ω_obs(t) = Observer’s render-phase intensity at time t
∇φ = Local phase gradient (fringe collapse / coherence)
τ_eff = Effective render delay (ms)
Δt_r = Observer-relative frame rate delta (ms)

Symbol Definitions

  • τ_eff – Effective render delay (linked to redshift, EEG phase-lag)
  • Ω_obs – Render field oscillation or anomaly flux
  • Ψ_f – BAO collapse threshold trigger
  • Δt_r – Temporal phase interval from observer frame
  • ρ_R – Render density (used in starfield prediction and cosmic drift)
  • v_sight – Speed of sight (visual render velocity)

Prediction Sample: Magnetic Field Drift

ΔB = Ω_obs · sin(τ_eff · f_solar)

Magnetic field anomalies can be forecasted by harmonics in the observer's render path. This model has been used to achieve over 95% accuracy in short-term magnetic variance forecasts.
